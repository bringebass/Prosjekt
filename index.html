What is webhosting?

Web hosting is basically to provide storage space and access to websites, and is essential to anyone who aims at having their own personal or business website. Web hosting firms are companies that provide this service to consumers or businesses - usually for a monthly service/subscription fee.The hosting services provided varies a great deal in terms of:

  • Performance - storage, bandwidth, RAM; essentially how fast your page will load and how many visitor per month you can have. Functionalities - added services that are free or paid for that help with everything from website management, increase speed, to making e-commerce and selling products easy from your website.
  • Functionalities - added services that are free or paid for that help with everything from website management, increase speed, to making e-commerce and selling products easy from your website.
  • Templates and user friendliness - the different hosting offers vary a lot in terms of how easy and intuitive their services are. Many providers have a website builder or templates where you can create your own website without knowing WordPress, HTML/Java or other coding languages. Also, the server administration can be easy, e.g. with a dashboard functionality like cPanel, or with more complexity, like larger servers that you manage by yourself. Note that there is often a trade-off between easiness and functionality - e.g. using a template means that you can easily create your site in a few minutes, however, that website is much more restricted in layout, format and functionality than a WordPress or HTML created website.
  • Support - which can vary between office hours Monday to Friday, to 24/7/365. It can also vary between channels (phone, chat and email), language options, and priority (do only premium customers get extensive support and how skilled are the support staff)
  • Price, of course.
The web hosting companies all have different packages trying to cover whatever needs their customers have - so you should find a hosting package that suits your needs. Usually the firms separate between the different types of web hosting:
  • Shared hosting - multiple consumers share a server that is being managed by the hosting companies. This is usually the cheapest and easiest way of hosting, but is usually restricted in terms of performance (speed and how many visitors per month), functionalities (limits on what you can install), support (limited and not prioritized). That said, shared servers can be good for an individual just starting a small website.
  • Virtual Private Server (VPS-hosting) - this is a more powerful version of the first option,but here the customers are promised a specific amount of server resources. The server is typically shared between a lower number of customers, allowing more performance for these customers, than with the typical shared hosting solution. This option is good for web sites that have outgrown Shared hosting and need more resources.
  • Dedicated servers - consumers purchase access to a specific server the service provider’s server park. Customers using dedicated server will have a specific fixed amount of server resources, and can chose between maintaining the server themselves or in having it ‘managed’ by the service provider.
  • Cloud servers - is in many ways the largest and most flexible solution, where customers do not buy capacity from a single server but from a network of servers. The capacity that is needed can be adjusted up and down depending on the needs of the customer’s website, at a click of a button, without the risk of shutting the website down during periods with excessive amounts of website visitors.

Types of webhosting

  • Shared hosting: The budget webhotel. Share your server with other users at a lower rate.
  • Virtual private server (VPS) hosting: The higher end hotel, with your own utilities at a higher rate.
  • Dedicated server hosting: The most expensive option, which gives you complete ownership of the host.
  • Cloud hosting: This type of hosting makes resources available through several cloud servers and is generally hosting the application or website through a network of cloud servers.
  • Managed hosting: here you outsource the management of your application, and instead of just paying for the server hosting, you pay additionally for services such as 24/7 support, updates and maintenance of your application or website.
  • Colocation: A physical data centre that allows for you to use your own hardware to host a server, while they provide the space and maintenance for a fee.
